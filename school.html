Here is something about my school from their website:

The Zochova 9 electrotechnical industrial school is located in the center of Bratislava, in the Staré mesto district with excellent transport connections. It is the school with the longest tradition among the schools of the same focus (SPŠE) in Bratislava.

The school building itself begins to "write its history" in 1910. This monumental building with a large courtyard is located on a plot whose area is today defined by Zochova, Podjavorinská, Palisády and Svoradova streets. It was built by master builder Salzleiter in the Art Nouveau architectural style with a noticeable Wagnerian influence. The object is included in the list of cultural monuments.

The history of the school begins in the school year 1949/1950, when it existed as an electrical engineering department of the Higher Industrial School at Fajnorov nábreží 5 in Bratislava. Its seat was the former Faculty of Natural Sciences of the Charles University on Markovičová street. These places now house the Supreme Court building. On the basis of the founding decree of the Commissioner for Education, Sciences and Arts dated November 23, 1950, on December 1, 1950, the electrical engineering department was separated from the Higher Industrial School as an independent Higher Industrial School of Electrical Engineering in the building of the former town school on Zochová street.

Our school has built a good name since its birth and gradually added tradition to it thanks to excellent teachers and graduates prepared for life.
